A good wine glass doesn’t have to cost a week’s groceries. The shape, thinness of the rim, and weight of the glass directly affect how the wine flows and how you perceive its aromas and flavors. For the price of a single high-end stem, you can often buy a whole set of well-designed budget glasses that lift your daily drinking without breaking the bank. When shopping on a budget, focus on three things: rim finish (thinner is better), stability (a sturdy base prevents tipping), and dishwasher safety. Crystal or tempered glass pairs durability with a refined feel. We’ve looked at owner feedback and professional reviews to find the three sets that best balance cost and performance for red and white drinkers alike.
