The shape of a wine glass isn’t just about looks, it fundamentally affects how the wine hits your palate, how the aromas develop, and how long the finish lingers. A well-designed bowl directs the wine to the right part of your tongue, while a thin rim lets you sip rather than spill. The right glass can elevate a bottle into something that tastes far more expensive. When choosing a set, start with what you drink most. If you mostly drink reds, a generous bowl with a wide opening (like those designed for Bordeaux or Burgundy) will give the wine room to breathe. For whites and sparkling, narrower bowls preserve chill and focus the aromatics. Stemmed glasses keep hand heat away from the bowl; stemless designs are less fragile and easier to store. You’ll also want to consider machine-made vs. hand-blown, the former is far more durable and affordable, the latter delivers thinner, more delicate rims.
